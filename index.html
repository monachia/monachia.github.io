Rainy Day Rush - Platformer

Controls: A, D to move. W/Space to jump. Mouse for umbrella.

Run, jump, and glide your way through this 2d side scrolling platformer with nothing but your trusty umbrella as you try to make it to your interview. But be careful, you won't get that job easily; there are dangers lurking around every corner. Brave the weather and keep your suit clean to look your best for the interview. Hope you get the job!

I specifically focused on programming and level design. I implemented the projectile enemies, the umbrella rotations, and the moving platforms. For level design, I worked a lot on the start area of level 2 and also parts of level 3.

NES Zelda Dungeon Recreation

Controls: WASD for movement. X for Sword and Z for alternate weapons. Invincibility by pressing 1. Secret level by pressing 4

I, along with my partner, recreated the first dungeon in NES Legend of Zelda. We both worked on different enemies and parts of the game. I focused on the projectile enemies, such as Goriya and Aquamentus. I worked a lot on the map layout and triggers, to help navigate through the world. Last but not least, I focused heavily on the secret mechanic.

Personal Game Engine

Over the course of 3 months, I worked on and built a 2d game engine that supports lua scripting. I personally worked on a custom skeletal animation system, which lets you break down an animation by key frames and control how each bone of an actor should move while an animation is playing.

Libraries/Technologies used: C++, Lua, SDL, GLM, Box2D, RapidJson, LuaBridge

Triangulation - Horror Game

Controls: WASD for movement. Mouse to look around. E to interact

Over the course of 2 weeks, I create a small horror game from scratch. The game is a survival game featuring no combat. You must make your way through the levels while escaping from the threats that surround you with nothing but your handy flashlight.
